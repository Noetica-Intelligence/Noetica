## 11. Landing Page Copy

**Hero Title:** 
Structure the Evolution of Knowledge.

**Hero Subtitle:** 
An open-source scientific intelligence network. Evaluate literature based on evidence, discover causal connections, and map cross-disciplinary research without relying on popularity metrics.

**CTA Buttons:** 
[ Apply for Closed Beta ]   [ View GitHub Repository ]

**Features:**
1. **Evidence-Based Ranking:** The engine evaluates findings based on foundational validity and signal integrity, not citation counts.
2. **Causal Knowledge Graphs:** Connect discoveries across disciplines. Visualize how a mechanism in one field impacts an outcome in another.
3. **Explainable Architecture:** Every ranking and connection is transparent. The system shows exactly how and why a conclusion was drawn.
4. **Open-Source Infrastructure:** Built for absolute transparency. Audit the code, run it locally, and contribute to the core algorithms.

**Benefits:**
- **Find what matters:** Uncover structurally sound research that traditional search engines bury.
- **Understand the context:** Read causal explanations of how discoveries link together, saving hours of manual literature review.
- **Trust the output:** Rely on a system that prioritizes scientific integrity over engagement algorithms. 

**FAQ Section:**
*Q: Is Noetica an AI chatbot?*
A: No. Noetica is an infrastructure project. It does not generate conversational responses; it evaluates and structures existing scientific literature into a continuous knowledge graph.

*Q: Who is the beta for?*
A: The closed beta is for researchers, scientists, bioinformaticians, and developers willing to test an experimental system and provide technical feedback.

*Q: Why open source?*
A: Scientific evaluation cannot be a black box. If an engine is ranking the validity of biological research, the underlying code must be auditable by the scientific community.

*Q: Will it always be free?*
A: The core engine and knowledge graph architecture will always remain open-source and free for individual researchers.

## 19. Community Guidelines

**Noetica Community Principles**

This community exists to support the development of open-source scientific infrastructure. 

1. **Evidence First:** Base your feedback, feature requests, and discussions on data and structural logic. 
2. **Constructive Friction:** Challenge the architecture, challenge the scoring algorithms, and challenge my assumptions. Do so respectfully and with evidence.
3. **No Hype:** Do not exaggerate the capabilities of this engine. Acknowledge limitations openly.
4. **Transparency:** Disclose any conflicts of interest if you are requesting features for commercial applications.
5. **Zero Tolerance:** Harassment, discrimination, or toxic behavior will result in immediate removal. We are here to build, not to argue.
