03 / BioSignal Scoring MATHEMATICAL MODELS

Noetica separates the signal from the noise using a combination of TF-IDF extraction, temporal momentum, and PageRank network centrality executed at bare-metal speeds in Zig.

The Problem with Keyword Matching

Traditional keyword alerts (e.g., Google Scholar Alerts) simply check if a word exists in a document. This leads to massive false-positive rates when the keyword is merely cited in passing. Noetica calculates the mathematical weight of the concept within the topology of the text.

PageRank Centrality

Within the Zig engine, Noetica constructs a graph where nodes represent documents and edges represent conceptual similarities (derived via term frequency vectors).

The algorithm iteratively computes the PageRank $PR(u)$ for each document $u$:

$$ PR(u) = \frac{1-d}{N} + d \sum_{v \in B(u)} \frac{PR(v)}{L(v)} $$

Where $d$ is the damping factor (typically 0.85), $B(u)$ is the set of documents strongly related to $u$, and $L(v)$ is the number of out-links from $v$. A high PageRank score means a paper is not only novel but highly central to the cluster of discoveries fetched that day.

Momentum & Impact Multipliers

Papers are further penalized or boosted based on metadata:

  • Impact Factor: Papers from high-impact domains receive a baseline multiplier.
  • Temporal Decay: Older discoveries suffer exponential decay in significance unless they possess overwhelming citation momentum.
