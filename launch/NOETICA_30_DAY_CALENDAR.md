# Noetica 30-Day Launch Content Calendar

*This document contains Deliverable 20: The strategic 30-day content calendar spanning LinkedIn, X, Instagram, Discord, and GitHub following the initial launch.*

***

## Overview & Philosophy
**Pacing:** Calm and consistent. No desperation. No hype cycles.
**Theme:** Transparency. Share what breaks, what works, and the philosophical logic behind the architecture.
**Goal:** Convert initial interest into sustained community engagement by showing the reality of building open-source infrastructure.

---

## Week 1: The Foundation & The Problems
*Focus: Explaining why Noetica exists and onboarding the first 500 users.*

**Day 1 (Launch Day)**
- **LinkedIn / X / Instagram:** The flagship launch announcements (Deliverables 1, 3, 4, 8). 
- **Discord:** Welcome message and rules.
- **GitHub:** Publish v0.1.0 release notes and open the Discussion board.

**Day 2**
- **X (Thread):** Deep dive into the "Wrong Model" of scientific search. Explain why popularity metrics (citations) harm structural discovery.
- **Discord:** First check-in. Ask the community what datasets they are currently trying to ingest.
- **GitHub:** Triage the first wave of bug reports. Label issues aggressively (`bug`, `enhancement`, `good first issue`).

**Day 3**
- **LinkedIn:** Short text post about open-source philosophy. Why scientific infrastructure cannot be a closed system. 
- **Instagram (Story):** Screenshot of the terminal/engine running. "Processing the first 1,000 queries from the beta cohort."

**Day 5**
- **X (Tweet):** "The hardest part of building Noetica so far has been evaluating causal confidence without hallucinating connections. Here is how the scoring algorithm handles it..." (Include a code snippet).
- **Discord / GitHub:** Post a summary of the top 3 bugs found this week and the timeline for fixing them.

---

## Week 2: Architecture & Transparency
*Focus: Getting technical. Showing the internals of the engine to build credibility with developers and bioinformaticians.*

**Day 8**
- **LinkedIn / X (Cross-post):** An architectural breakdown of the knowledge graph. Explain why Noetica uses graphs instead of category trees for cross-disciplinary research.
- **Instagram (Carousel):** 5 slides showing the data pipeline from "Raw Literature" to "Ranked Discovery."

**Day 10**
- **X (Thread):** A transparent look at what broke in Week 1. "The ingestion pipeline failed on X. Here is why, and here is the patch I pushed to GitHub."
- **Discord:** Ask for specific feedback on the UI/UX of the Discovery Timeline. 

**Day 12**
- **GitHub Discussions:** Start a thread on feature prioritization. Ask the community to vote on the next major architectural update.
- **Instagram (Story):** A time-lapse of writing documentation or reviewing a community Pull Request.

**Day 14 (End of Week 2)**
- **Email / Discord:** Send the mid-beta feedback survey (Deliverable 16) to the 500 testers.
- **LinkedIn:** A brief update on the beta. "Two weeks in. 500 users. 43 bugs reported. 12 fixed. The open-source model is working."

---

## Week 3: The Philosophy of Evidence
*Focus: Highlighting the core mission—evidence over popularity—and showcasing early wins from the community.*

**Day 15**
- **X (Tweet):** "In traditional academia, a paper's value is often measured by its journal impact factor. In Noetica, it is measured by its structural validity. We have to change the metrics."

**Day 17**
- **LinkedIn:** Case study based on user feedback. Share an example (anonymized if necessary) of how a beta tester used Noetica to find a cross-disciplinary connection that Google Scholar missed. 
- **Discord:** Host a 30-minute text-based AMA in a specific channel to discuss the scoring algorithms.

**Day 19**
- **Instagram (Post):** The 8 Core Questions checklist. Visual graphic explaining how the engine evaluates a paper.
- **GitHub:** Push v0.1.1. Release notes focusing on stability and performance optimizations based on Week 1 and 2 feedback.

**Day 21**
- **X (Thread):** Explain the concept of "BioSignal Integrity." Break down the math or the logic behind how Noetica scores discoveries.

---

## Week 4: The Roadmap & The Future
*Focus: Transitioning from an early beta to a long-term open-source project.*

**Day 23**
- **LinkedIn:** "Building solo vs. building open source." A reflection on the past month of managing contributions, reviewing PRs, and learning from 500 researchers. 
- **Discord:** Open a discussion about expanding the beta cohort. "Is the infrastructure ready for 1,000 more users?"

**Day 25**
- **X (Tweet):** "Feature requests are a great indicator of what researchers actually need. In the last 3 weeks, 80% of users asked for [Feature]. I am building it into v0.2.0."
- **Instagram (Story):** Roadmap preview. A clean, minimal checklist of what is coming next month.

**Day 28**
- **GitHub Discussions:** Publish the formal v0.2.0 Roadmap. Outline what features were rejected and explain the philosophical reasons why (e.g., rejecting citation counters).

**Day 30 (Beta Conclusion / Next Phase)**
- **All Platforms (LinkedIn, X, Email):** The 30-Day Report. 
  * "One month ago, I opened Noetica to 500 researchers."
  * Share transparent metrics (number of queries run, bugs fixed, PRs merged).
  * Announce the next steps: opening the waitlist to the next 1,000 users.
  * Reiterate the mission: Evidence over popularity.

---

## Daily Operational Cadence
- **Morning (30 mins):** Review GitHub Issues. Triage bugs. 
- **Mid-day (15 mins):** Check Discord. Answer specific technical questions. Do not feel obligated to maintain constant chatter.
- **Afternoon (30 mins):** Review open Pull Requests. Merge or provide architectural feedback.
- **Evening:** Write and schedule the social content for the following day. 

*Note: Consistency is more important than volume. If there is nothing valuable to say on a given day, say nothing.*
