## 1. Flagship LinkedIn Launch Post (Long-form)

The architecture of scientific discovery is broken. 

Over the last decade, we have digitized human knowledge, but we have not structured it. Every day, thousands of research papers are published. To navigate this volume, we rely on search engines and citation graphs that are fundamentally designed to optimize for popularity, not structural truth. 

If a paper is highly cited, it surfaces. If a discovery is structurally sound but buried in a niche journal, it disappears into the noise. We have built systems that reward engagement rather than evidence.

For the past year, I have been working on a solution to this. Today, I am opening the closed beta for Noetica.

Noetica is an open-source scientific intelligence network. It is not an AI wrapper, and it is not a traditional paper search engine. It is an infrastructure project designed to map how human knowledge evolves across disciplines.

I built Noetica on a core philosophy:
- Evidence over popularity.
- Discoveries over publications.
- Explainability over black-box decisions.

When you query Noetica, the input is not just your interest—it is your expertise, your reading constraints, and your research goals. The output is a ranked list of discoveries, structural summaries, causal evaluations, and cross-field connections. It evaluates findings based on foundational validity and signal integrity, building a continuous knowledge graph rather than isolating information in category trees.

This is the first public beta. It is an early, experimental release. The ingestion pipelines work, the baseline scoring algorithms are functional, but the platform is incomplete. 

I am releasing this early because scientific infrastructure cannot be built in a vacuum, and it cannot be a black box. If an engine is evaluating the viability of a biological pathway, the code making that decision must be transparent. That is why Noetica is completely open-source.

I am opening the closed beta today. I am looking for researchers, bioinformaticians, developers, and scientists who are willing to test the system, tear apart the architecture, and tell me exactly where it fails. I need early testers who will report bugs, challenge my assumptions, and help shape the long-term roadmap. 

If you are interested in building open infrastructure for scientific discovery, I invite you to join the beta.

**Title:** I built an open-source scientific intelligence network to rank literature by evidence, not citations (Early Beta)

Hi everyone,

For the past year, I’ve been building Noetica as a solo founder. Today, I am opening the closed beta and open-sourcing the architecture. 

**The Problem**
Current tools for scientific discovery are fundamentally citation graphs. They optimize for popularity. This creates echo chambers and leaves structurally sound, cross-disciplinary findings buried in the noise. We have digitized knowledge, but we haven't structured it efficiently.

**What I Built**
Noetica is an open-source intelligence network. It is not an AI wrapper or a conversational chatbot. It is an engine that evaluates literature based on foundational validity, signal integrity, and causal confidence. 

Instead of category trees, it uses a knowledge graph to map cross-disciplinary connections. Instead of returning a list of PDFs based on keywords, it returns ranked discoveries and structural explanations. 

**The Current State**
This is v0.1.0-beta. It is experimental. The ingestion pipelines are functional but unoptimized for massive datasets. The baseline scoring algorithms work, but they require validation against complex biological data. 

**Why I'm Posting Here**
Scientific infrastructure cannot be built in a vacuum, and it cannot be closed-source. I am looking for researchers, bioinformaticians, and developers to test the system, review the code, and give me brutally honest feedback. I need people to break the engine and tell me where the architecture fails.
